Mount Everest

Mount Everest is the highest mountain on Earth, located in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and China (Tibet). It stands at an impressive height of 8,848 meters (29,029 feet) above sea level.

Amazon Rainforest

The Amazon Rainforest is the largest tropical rainforest in the world, covering a vast area of approximately 5.5 million square kilometers (2.1 million square miles). It is located primarily in Brazil but also extends into other South American countries.

Great Barrier Reef

The Great Barrier Reef is the world's largest coral reef system, stretching over 2,300 kilometers (1,400 miles) along the northeastern coast of Australia. It is home to an incredible diversity of marine life and is a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Sahara Desert

The Sahara Desert is the largest hot desert in the world, spanning across North Africa. It covers an area of approximately 9.2 million square kilometers (3.6 million square miles), which is comparable to the size of the United States.

Nile River

The Nile River is the longest river in the world, flowing northwards through northeastern Africa for about 6,650 kilometers (4,130 miles). It passes through several countries, including Egypt, Sudan, South Sudan, Uganda, and Ethiopia.

Ring of Fire

The Ring of Fire is a major area in the basin of the Pacific Ocean where a large number of earthquakes and volcanic eruptions occur. It is characterized by a horseshoe-shaped ring of intense seismic and volcanic activity, encompassing countries such as Japan, Indonesia, Chile, and the United States.

Dead Sea

The Dead Sea, located between Jordan and Israel, is the lowest point on land, with its surface sitting about 430 meters (1,410 feet) below sea level. It is known for its extremely high salt concentration, which allows swimmers to effortlessly float on its surface.
